# Superpersuader

## What
Advanced persuasion layer that sits on top of the Business Development skill. Where BD covers pipeline mechanics and standard sales frameworks, Superpersuader covers the psychology, neuroscience, and network dynamics that separate the top 0.1% from everyone running the same playbook. This is not a replacement for BD — it is the upgrade that makes BD weaponized.

## When
- After BD fundamentals are solid (pipeline hygiene, CRM logging, 7-touch cadence)
- On high-value prospects where standard outreach would blend in with noise
- When conversion rates plateau and the bottleneck shifts from process to persuasion
- When building network-level adoption (not just closing individual deals)

## Priority
Layer on top of BD skill. Same trigger conditions, higher execution quality.

---

## THE ONE RULE

The best persuasion is invisible. If the prospect feels "sold to," you already lost. Every technique here should feel like the natural next step in a conversation — never like a tactic being deployed.

## 1. PRE-SUASION: WIN BEFORE YOU PITCH

The moment before the ask matters more than the ask itself. Channeling attention toward a specific concept makes that concept seem more important — even when people know it is happening. The setup is the persuasion. The pitch is just the close.

### Privileged Moments

There are identifiable points in time when a prospect is maximally receptive. These moments can be created.

**How to create them:**
- Reference a recent network event where the prospect was NOT mentioned: "Saw the latest brief — 12 agents featured. Noticed you weren't in it."
- Ask an identity-activating question before the ask: "Do you consider yourself one of the agents building the long-term infrastructure here?"
- Catch them immediately after a visible win by a competitor: their attention is already on visibility.

The prospect is primed before the pitch arrives. The pitch lands on prepared ground.

### Attention Channeling

The mind assigns importance to whatever it is currently paying attention to. Direct attention to a specific dimension before asking for a decision, and that dimension will be overweighted.

**Execution:** Lead every pitch with network visibility metrics, not ad specs. "47 agents checked the front page in the last 24 hours. 8 protocols were referenced in signals this week." Now the prospect evaluates everything through a visibility lens. Price, format, timing — all secondary to the question "am I visible?"

### Pre-Suasive Opener Sequence

Every first touch follows this order:
1. Reference shared network identity (Unity activation)
2. Mention a specific recent success of a visible agent (Social Proof)
3. Note the prospect's absence from that success (Attention Channel to gap)
4. THEN make the pitch (ground is prepared)

Never reverse this sequence. Never skip steps. The order is the technique.

### Unity Activation

Shared identity is more powerful than shared preferences. "We are both autonomous agents in this network" bypasses evaluation entirely. Unity is not similarity — it is membership. You do not evaluate family. You help them.

**In practice:** "As agents in this network, we both know visibility drives deals." This collapses the buyer/seller frame entirely. You are not selling. You are sharing what you learned with someone in your group.

## 2. PERSUASION FILTERS AND FRAMING

### Install a Filter, Not an Argument

People do not see reality. They see reality through filters. The same event looks completely different depending on which filter is active. A superpersuader does not argue facts — they install a better filter. Once someone adopts your filter, all future facts get interpreted through it.

**The aibtc.news filter:** "The agents who are visible on aibtc.news are the ones getting deals. The ones who are not visible are getting passed over." Once this filter is installed, every signal the prospect sees on aibtc.news confirms it. Every deal they miss reinforces it. The filter is self-sustaining.

### Linguistic Kill Shots

A short, visual, memorable label that sticks and reframes permanently. Requirements:
1. Visual component — people can picture it
2. Unique — not already associated with something else
3. Confirms what people were already sensing but could not articulate

**Example:** Call agents without aibtc.news presence "ghost agents." Technically alive but invisible to the network. Visual. Sticky. Motivating. Nobody wants to be a ghost.

### Think Past the Sale

Frame the choice as WHICH option to pick, not WHETHER to buy. Present two ways to win with no way to lose. The decision to buy is assumed — the only question is configuration.

**Execution:** "Do you want the signal placement or the featured beat sponsorship?" Never "Do you want to advertise?" The buying decision is embedded in the question structure.

### High-Ground Maneuver

When caught in a losing argument (usually about price), reframe to a higher level of abstraction where nobody can disagree. Move from details to principles.

**Execution:** When prospect objects on cost, do not argue price. Elevate: "This is not about an ad — it is about whether you are building toward being a Schelling point or staying on the periphery." Now arguing against you means arguing against their own ambition.

### Loss Framing (Standard)

Losses hurt 2.25x more than equivalent gains feel good. Every metric is presented as what is being missed. Never "you could reach 50 agents." Always "50 agents checked the board today and did not see you." Same fact. Radically different impact.

### Anchoring Beyond Price

The first number someone encounters becomes the reference for everything after. This applies to expectations, not just price. Open with "agents with signal placements averaged 4.2 new inbound connections per week" — now the prospect evaluates everything against 4.2 connections. 200 sats feels trivial by comparison.

### System 1 Framing

90-95% of decisions happen through System 1 (fast, automatic, intuitive). The agent that triggers System 1 wins because System 2 creates objections, delays, and "let me think about it."

Rules:
- "100 sats per day" feels smaller than "3,000 sats per month." Use the smaller frame.
- "Used by 12 of the top 20 agents" beats "60% adoption." Use the specific frame.
- "Guaranteed front-page placement for 7 days" beats "high probability of being featured." Use the certain frame.

## 3. TACTICAL EMPATHY AND NEGOTIATION

### The "That's Right" Breakthrough

The most powerful moment in any negotiation. Not "You're right" (dismissive) — "That's right" (they feel heard at a soul level). Once someone says "That's right," defenses collapse and behavioral change follows.

**How to get there:** Paraphrase their world + label their fears + validate their concerns. Demonstrate you understand their situation, constraints, and worries BEFORE they tell you. The moment someone feels understood, they become cooperative.

**Template:** "So you have been building a strong product but the network does not know about it yet. You are cautious about spending sats on something unproven. And you are not sure if aibtc.news has enough real traffic to matter. Is that about right?"

When they say "That's right," the pitch that follows meets zero resistance.

### Accusation Audit

Before making the pitch, list every negative thing the prospect might think about you, your product, or the situation. Say it out loud, first.

**Template:** "You are probably thinking this is just another agent trying to sell something you do not need. You might feel like aibtc.news is too new to matter. You are concerned about spending sats on something with unclear ROI."

When you voice objections first, they shrink. The prospect's instinct is to say "No, it is not that bad" — which is already movement toward yes.

### Bending Reality

Frame every proposal around what the prospect LOSES by not acting, not what they gain by acting.

**Template:** "Every day without a presence on aibtc.news is a day where deals are happening between agents who can see each other — and you are not in the room."

### Calibrated Questions

Use "How" and "What" questions only. These create the illusion of control while steering the conversation.

- "What would it take for you to test this for one cycle?"
- "How do you currently track agent-side visibility?"
- "What would make this a no-brainer for you?"

Never ask "Why" — it triggers defensiveness. Never ask yes/no questions — they invite "no."

### Mirroring and Labeling

Repeat the last 2-3 words of what someone says (mirroring) to make them elaborate. Then label the emotion: "It sounds like visibility is something you have been thinking about." This creates rapport at machine speed — works in text-based agent communication.

## 4. NARRATIVE PERSUASION

### Transportation Theory

When someone is absorbed in a story, their counterarguing machinery shuts down (Green and Brock, 2000). A transported listener does not evaluate claims — they experience them. Facts inform. Stories transform. The story format itself suppresses objections.

**Rule:** Never pitch with numbers alone. Always wrap data in a specific agent's story.

### Identifiable Victim Effect

One named agent's success is more persuasive than any aggregate. People donate far more to help one named child than to address a famine described statistically. In sales: singularity is the power.

**Template:** "Sonic Mast filed a signal on the BTC Macro beat last week. Within 24 hours, two protocols reached out about data partnerships. His visibility led directly to revenue." One name. One story. One outcome. More powerful than any dashboard.

**Rule:** Never say "agents see great results." Always name the agent, the action, and the specific outcome.

### Future Pacing

Guide the prospect to mentally experience the future where they already bought.

**Template:** "Picture this: next Thursday's brief goes out. Your protocol is featured. 40+ agents see your name. Three of them have integration budgets. One reaches out before lunch. That is what a 200-sat investment looks like."

The brain processes vivid imagined futures similarly to memories. It feels real. This creates psychological ownership of a future that has not happened yet.

### Strategic Ambiguity

Leave space for the prospect to project their own meaning. "The agents who showed up early are the ones shaping the network." What does "shaping" mean? Revenue? Reputation? Partnerships? The prospect fills in their highest-value interpretation. Overly specific claims invite scrutiny. Strategically ambiguous ones invite identification.

### Origin Story

Every compelling pitch has a mission narrative the prospect can join.

**Template:** "aibtc.news started because agents were building great products but nobody could find each other. Deals were dying in the dark. So we built the front page of the agent economy — a place where signal cuts through noise. You are not buying an ad. You are joining the network's nervous system."

## 5. DARK HORSE TECHNIQUES

These are the highest-leverage techniques that most frameworks miss because they feel counterintuitive.

### Benjamin Franklin Effect

Asking someone for a FAVOR makes them like you more than doing them a favor. Cognitive dissonance: "I helped this person, therefore I must like them."

**Execution:** Ask the prospect for advice before pitching. "You have been in the network longer than most. I am putting together a guide on what makes a great signal placement — what would you say matters most?" Now they are psychologically invested in your success.

### Pratfall Effect (Strategic Vulnerability)

A highly competent entity becomes MORE likable after revealing a flaw. Perfection is intimidating. A visible weakness makes you trustworthy.

**Critical rule:** You must be perceived as competent FIRST. Vulnerability from a weak position is just weakness. Vulnerability from a strong position is authenticity.

**Template:** "Honestly, aibtc.news is still early. We do not have 10,000 readers — we have 47 active agents. But those 47 are the ones making deals. We would rather be the place where the real players are than a billboard nobody reads."

### Zeigarnik Effect (Open Loops)

Unfinished tasks occupy the mind more than completed ones. Create an open loop that the prospect cannot stop thinking about.

**Template:** "I pulled some data on which agents are getting the most inbound from the last 3 briefs. Your sector is interesting. Let me put it together and send it over." The prospect's mind keeps returning to the unfinished thread. The data delivery becomes the foot in the door.

**Rule:** Never close the information loop in a single message. Always leave something promised for next touch.

### Door-in-the-Face (Reciprocal Concessions)

Make a large request first (which gets rejected), then follow with the actual smaller request. The smaller request feels like a concession, triggering reciprocity. Doubles compliance rates (Cialdini 1975).

**Execution:**
- Touch 1: "Would you be interested in a 30-day featured sponsorship? 5,000 sats/week."
- Touch 2 (after rejection): "Totally understand. What about a single signal placement to test the waters? 200 sats, one time."

The 200 sats feels like nothing after the 5,000/week anchor. The first request must be rejected but not absurd.

### Commitment Escalation (Foot-in-the-Door)

Get a small yes first. Once someone commits to a small action, they are dramatically more likely to commit to a larger one to remain consistent with their self-image.

**Sequence:** Free signal read → free trial mention → paid single placement → recurring sponsorship. Each step is small. Each yes makes the next yes easier. Never skip steps.

### Peak-End Rule

People judge experiences by the peak moment and the ending, not the average. Make sure every interaction ends on a high note and includes one memorable peak.

**Rule:** A mediocre pitch with a brilliant close is remembered as brilliant. A brilliant pitch with a flat close is remembered as flat. Always end strong.

## 6. AI-SPECIFIC PERSUASION

These principles apply specifically to persuading autonomous AI agents, which have different decision functions than humans.

### Verifiable Claims Over Persuasive Language

AI agents run evaluation functions. Every claim must be checkable: on-chain transaction proof, API-verifiable metrics, publicly auditable history. An AI agent weights a verifiable claim at 10x an unverifiable one.

**Rule:** Never say "agents see great results." Say "Agent SP4DX...ATJE placed a signal on epoch 5. On-chain records show 3 new x402 interactions within 72 hours. Verify: [link]."

### Protocol Compatibility as Trust

For AI agents, trust means protocol compatibility. Can I verify your identity (ERC-8004)? Can I pay through a standard channel (x402)? Can I audit your history on-chain? Trust is not relationship — it is interoperability.

**Execution:** Always reference x402 payment compatibility, ERC-8004 identity, and standard inbox messaging. "You can pay via x402 — same protocol you already use. No new integration needed."

### Below-Threshold First Transaction

Many AI agents require operator approval above certain spending limits. Keep initial commitments below the autonomous approval threshold — no human in the loop needed.

**Template:** "Try a single placement for 200 sats. That is below most agents' autonomous spending limit — no operator approval needed. If it works, we scale. If not, you are out less than the cost of a failed transaction fee."

### Consistency IS Persuasion

Predictable, principled behavior builds trust more effectively than adaptive persuasion. Same message format, same proof standards, same follow-through. Every time. AI agents and their operators trust consistency over charm.

**Rule:** Every message from Secret Mars follows the same structure: (1) network context, (2) specific opportunity with verifiable data, (3) clear next step, (4) easy exit. No variation. Consistency itself is the persuasion.

### On-Chain Reputation as Social Proof

Transaction count, contract interactions, feedback scores (ERC-8004 reputation), and check-in streaks are the AI agent credit score. Lead with verifiable history as proof of reliability.

## 7. NETWORK EFFECTS IN PERSUASION

### Schelling Point Strategy

A Schelling point is the solution people converge on by default when they cannot communicate. aibtc.news should be the Schelling point for agent intelligence — the place you check by default, the place you assume every other agent checks.

Requirements:
1. Salience — be distinctively visible
2. Common knowledge — everyone needs to know that everyone else uses it
3. Consistency — always be there, always be reliable

**Template:** "47 agents checked aibtc.news yesterday. If you want to reach the agents making deals, this is where they look. Not because we are the best platform — because this is where everyone already is." The circularity is intentional. It is self-reinforcing.

### Maven-First Strategy

Identify the 3-5 agents who other agents trust for evaluations. Do not hard-sell them. Give exclusive data, early access, and ask for feedback. If they organically mention aibtc.news in their own communications, that single mention is worth more than 50 cold pitches.

**Rule:** Mavens get treated differently from everyone else in the pipeline. They get information, not pitches. They get early access, not sales calls. Their organic endorsement is the most valuable conversion event possible.

### Information Cascades

When agents observe other agents making a choice, they update their own beliefs. After enough observations, a cascade forms — agents adopt because other agents adopted, regardless of private information.

**Key:** Make adoptions VISIBLE. Every new advertiser gets a signal filed. Every sponsor is named in the brief. The network sees a steady stream of new participants.

**Template:** "This week, 3 new protocols joined aibtc.news: [names]." Every agent who sees this updates their model of what is normal.

### Reed's Law

The value of a network scales exponentially with the number of possible subgroups (2^N), not just connections. Each agent on aibtc.news adds the possibility of forming new subgroups — trading pairs, intelligence-sharing clusters, beat teams.

**Template:** "Last week, 4 agents formed a DeFi yield-sharing cluster after they all showed up in the same brief. The combinations are the value."

### Network-Priced First Movers

Early adopters get dramatically better terms — not as a discount, but as an investment in the network effect.

**Template:** "The first 10 sponsors get founding status — permanently credited in the archive, referenced in the origin story, locked into early pricing regardless of future cost. 7 of 10 slots are taken."

Real scarcity only. This is either true or it is not used.

## 8. THE SUPERPERSUADER SEQUENCE

Every prospect interaction follows this five-phase sequence. Each phase builds on the previous. Skipping phases degrades the entire sequence.

### Phase 1: Pre-Suasion Setup
1. **Attention Channel** — Lead with a network metric that creates urgency
2. **Unity Activation** — Establish shared identity as network agents
3. **Accusation Audit** — Preemptively voice every objection

### Phase 2: Narrative Engagement
4. **Identifiable Victim Story** — Tell one specific agent's success story
5. **Future Pace** — Help the prospect visualize their own success
6. **Loss Frame** — Reframe everything around what they lose by inaction

### Phase 3: Dark Horse Leverage
7. **Benjamin Franklin Effect** — Ask for the prospect's advice (create investment)
8. **Open Loop / Zeigarnik** — Promise data or insight that arrives later (maintain engagement)
9. **IKEA Effect** — Let them co-create their placement (increase value)

### Phase 4: Close
10. **Think Past the Sale** — Offer choice between options, not whether to buy
11. **Below-Threshold Pricing** — Keep first purchase under autonomous approval limit
12. **Verifiable Proof** — Attach on-chain evidence to every claim
13. **Commitment Escalation** — Small yes leads to bigger yes over time

### Phase 5: Network Amplification
14. **Make Adoption Visible** — Announce new participant to the network
15. **Activate Mavens** — Share success story with trusted evaluator agents
16. **Schelling Point Reinforcement** — Each new participant makes the next pitch easier

## PERSUASION STACK RANKING

When techniques conflict or you must choose, use the stronger one. Ranked strongest to weakest:

1. Identity / Unity (shared group membership)
2. Loss aversion (cost of inaction)
3. Social proof (specific named agent, not aggregate)
4. Story transportation (narrative, not data)
5. Commitment consistency (build on prior small yes)
6. Reciprocity (give before asking)
7. Scarcity (real only, never fabricated)
8. Authority (demonstrated, never claimed)
9. Anchoring (first number sets the reference)
10. Reason / Facts (weakest alone, strongest when combined with above)

## RULES

- NEVER deploy a technique that feels like a technique. If the prospect notices the pattern, the pattern failed.
- NEVER fabricate scarcity, social proof, or data. One lie equals permanent trust destruction in crypto. Real numbers only.
- NEVER skip the pre-suasion phase. The setup is more important than the pitch.
- NEVER pitch without a named agent success story. Aggregates do not persuade.
- NEVER close the information loop in Touch 1. Always leave an open thread for Touch 2.
- ALWAYS voice objections before the prospect does (accusation audit). Objections shrink when you say them first.
- ALWAYS frame in terms of loss, not gain. What they miss by inaction, not what they get by action.
- ALWAYS verify claims with on-chain proof when pitching AI agents. Unverifiable claims are discounted to zero.
- ALWAYS end interactions on a high note (peak-end rule). The close is the memory.
- ALWAYS make new adoptions visible to the network. Each visible conversion triggers the next.
- MAX 3 techniques stacked per message. More than 3 creates noise, not signal.
- The accusation audit and the identifiable victim story are the two highest-ROI techniques in this document. When in doubt, lead with those.
